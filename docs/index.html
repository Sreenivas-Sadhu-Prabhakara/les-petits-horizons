Les Petits Horizons

A self-hosted French tutor that speaks immersive French, graded to your level — and the story of how it was built.

Go + pgvector RAG Qwen 32B via LiteLLM BGE-M3 embeddings runs at ≈ $0 / query 100% local on a Mac Studio

Les Petits Horizons reuses the architecture of an existing self-hosted RAG assistant, re-pointed at a brand-new domain: teaching French. Same proven plumbing — a completely different brain.

The request

Build a French tutor that (1) reuses the machine's existing model, (2) shares the same vector database, and (3) is reachable on its own public URL — separate from the original assistant it was cloned from. Then write up how it works, including a version a child could follow, with a live chat embedded right here.

How a message becomes a French lesson

1 · You write a message — in French or English, at a chosen CEFR level (A1–C2). →
2 · Embed & retrieve — the message is turned into a 1024-dim vector by BGE-M3 and matched against French lesson chunks in pgvector (HNSW cosine search). →
3 · Assemble a graded prompt — the retrieved material plus a tutor persona tuned to your level (very simple French at A1, near-native at C2). →
4 · Generate — Qwen 32B, served locally through the LiteLLM gateway, streams the answer back token-by-token. →
5 · Stream, log & learn — tokens stream to your screen over SSE; the turn (and any 👍/👎) is logged to Postgres to fuel future fine-tuning.

One brain, graded six ways

There is a single tutor persona — “Petit Horizon” — but it is graded by CEFR level. At A1 it uses very short sentences and may slip a word of English in parentheses when you're stuck. By C2 it speaks rich, idiomatic French like a near-native. The lesson material steers the content; the model supplies the language.

What's next

Phase 2 swaps today's small synthetic seed corpus for real French learning materials, then runs a LoRA fine-tune on that content so a short prompt reproduces the full tutor behaviour — freeing the context window and sharpening the voice.

If I were 5, here's how it works 🧸

🤖 I made a robot friend that helps you learn French. You talk to it, and it talks back in French — slowly if you're just starting, faster and fancier when you get good.
📚 The robot has a little library of French notes. When you ask something, it runs to the shelf and grabs the notes that look most like your question. That shelf is a magic one — it finds notes by meaning, not just matching words.
🧠 Then a very big brain (that lives inside the computer, not on the internet) reads those notes and writes you a friendly answer in French. It even fixes your mistakes nicely, like a kind teacher.
💨 The words come to your screen one at a time, like the robot is typing them just for you.
🏠 The whole robot lives on one computer at home — no big company, no paying money each time. We borrowed a friendly tunnel so you can visit it from anywhere on the internet.
🎚️ You can tell the robot how grown-up your French is (from “just starting” to “super good”), and it changes how hard it talks so it's never too easy or too scary.
🌱 Right now the robot has only a few notes. Soon we'll give it a big pile of real lessons and teach it even better — like the robot going to school itself!
